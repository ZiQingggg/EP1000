A Whistle Ring(Individual Assignment)

The Whistle Ring it is a safety defense whistle ring meant for children and women of all ages. This whistle ring idea came to me from my friend who had dealt with an bad experience as a child so thus I decided to create this product and at the same time also felt that 3D printing would be a better way to create this object .How I created this product was first of starting by generating out a basic sketch first

-After that I went to the internet to scour out how to create a whistle first, which I then came upon a website where it contained instructables(https://www.instructables.com/3D-Printing-Whistle/) on how to create a whistle so then I decided to it use it as reference to help me create my whistle part of the ring and even though they were using a different software I then decided to tweak it a bit for my own version of a normal whistle to helping me combine with the ring part.

-So essentially first I started out by generating the basic outline of it using the rectangle tool and the circle tool

-Subsequently I then click on finish sketch and extrude out the base first

-Next create a new sketch on the top surface again and used line tool and offset tool to create the rim of the whistle and made it will be 2mm in thickness and that there is angled part near the whistle hole.

-Next I then clicked on finish sketch and extruded out the rim making sure it was offset by 2mm, operation was changed to join and extruded out as 10mm

-Next I then click on the base sketch and extruded a top surface and made sure to offset it by 12 mm, operation was changed to join and extruded out as 2mm to close up the whistle

-Subsequently I then went to measure the diameter of my ring finger so it will fit perfectly

-Then next I went back to fusion 360 and create a new sketch

-Next I used circle tool and offset tool to create the outline for the ring

-After that I then click on finish sketch and extruded out the ring making sure it was offsetted and extruded out as 8mm.

-Next I then went in the create tab I selected the sphere, clicked on the top surface so I can create the sphere in the center of the whistle.

-After that I went to the side view and made sure the ball is connected to the whistle by 0.2mm so that after 3d printing it out I can just use a sharp object to break the ball of in the whistle I won't need to complicate myself.

-Next I then Select the whistle body and sphere body under modify I click on combine to merge them into one object.

-After that I selected the edges of the whistle internally and eternally and fillet them making sure the corner type is in rolling ball and tangent was at a 0.5mm so as to smoothen it out.

-Next I also fillet the internal edges of the ring making sure the corner type is in rolling ball and tangent was at a 0.3mm

-As for the external edges of the ring I decided to chamfer them at 0.5mm to give it a more slick look.

-Next for more decoration I then create a new sketch on the top surface

-Used a line tool and create out a crown outline

-After that I then click on finish sketch and started extruding inwards and making sure it was only at 0.2mm.

-Next I then did it again for the other side.

-And thats it
